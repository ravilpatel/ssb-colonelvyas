Types of Sequences or Series

  • > Numericals- Use of Numbers
  • > Alphanumeric- Use of Alphabets plus Numericals


Important points to note and solve questions in seconds!

You must remember Number-Alphabet Ranking for fast solving of Alphanumeric Questions.


Questions can be framed on following type-

> Addition and Subtraction of particular number
 Eg: 1,8,15,22,29…. (Addition of 7)

> Multiplication or Division of particular number
 Eg: 8, 16, 32, 64,... (Multiplication by 2)
308, 108, 36, 12, 4… (Multiplication by 13)

> Logical Pattern
 Eg: 2,3,5,7,11,13,17… (Series of Prime Numbers)
12, 14, 16, 18 … (This is harmonic Sequence, Reciprocal of 2,4,6,8…)

> Alphabetic Sequence
 Eg: A, G, M, S, Y, E…. (Converting Alphabet into number)
(You must try to check and satisfy the condition accordingly).


Solved Examples

  1. 1. Find the next letter in the series
        D, G, J, M, P, ____.

1.

Q

2.

R

3.

S

4.

T

Solution: 

Three letters are added to each letter to get the next letter in the series.

D+3, G+3, J+3, M+3, P+3, S


  1. 2. Find the next letter in the series
       A, B, D, H, ____.

1.

L

2.

N

3.

R

4.

P

Solution:
Each letter in the given series is multiplied with 2 to get the next letter in the series.

A × 2 ⇒ 1 × 2 = 2 and the 2nd letter is B,

B × 2 ⇒ 2 × 2 = 4 and the 4th letter is D.

Similarly, H × 2 ⇒ 8 × 2 = 16 and the 16th letter is P.


  1. 3. Find the missing number.

   25 : 36 : : 49 : _____.

1.

61

2.

63

3.

65

4.

60

Solution:

When the numbers in the question are considered the students tend to consider 25 and 36 as squares of two consecutive natural numbers. But the answer choices do not consist of an answer suitable to the above logic. Hence, it is important that the student keeps the answer choices in view in arriving at the logic.

25 + 11 = 36

Similarly, 49 + 11 = 60
